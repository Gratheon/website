Problems we are solving

💡 Hard, frequent, unscalable work to keep bees healthy for precise pollination and food security

Bees are key species for our food security. Farmers are cooperating with beekeepers to combat 🌻 Precise pollination and increase yields

But traditional beekeeping requires 👁️ Observability of bee colony, because bees can experience 🍽️ Bee colony starvation , 💀 Colony without a queen, 🧶 Swarming, 🦀 Infestations and 🦀 Diseases which weaken the colony and cause ❄️ Overwintering collapse.

Observability is typically solved with a 💪🏻 Physical labor and 👁️ Time-consuming inspections. Dressed in a protective suit, in the summer heat, every week, you need to lift 20 kg beehive sections designed 150 years ago and notice small details while you are getting stung by defending bees, 💀 Intrusive inspections.

As your apiary grows, this becomes 💪🏻 Unscalable work. 👨🏻‍🚒 Industrial beekeepers have 🏘️ Multiple apiaries at 🌲 Distant locations that are hard to reach. Existing solutions do not adequately solve these issues

On “saving the bees”​

We are not focusing solely on saving honeybees as a species, because globally colony numbers are increasing. Ecosystems are complex. We support diversity of plants and wild pollinators.
