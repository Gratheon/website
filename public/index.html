Why Gratheon

Honeybees are cute super-organisms that are key to natural pollination and our food supply.

Traditional hobby beekeeping involves hard physical labor and constant monitoring to keep track of bee colony state. Manual inspections do not scale well with the amount of colonies and growth of the hive. When you are in a suit, you have very limited in time to go through all of the frames, as colony can get stressed out.

Monitoring is needed to help colonies survive some of the factors. Highest risk factos are varroa mite infestation, hornet attacks, bears, late swarming, robbing state, wax moth infestation, rodents, monocultural crops, viral and bacterial infections, pesticide use.

Without proper technological solutions, most beekeepers to this day rely on using medieval alcohol washing tests that kill hundreds of bees to get level of mite infestation.

Existing modern beehive monitoring solutions on the market are inadequate because they focus on low-cost temperature and sound sensors that provide insufficient information. Some solutions focus only on one area - counting mites or detecting queen, forgetting the big picture.

We can do better

Our long-term vision is a single-colony autonomous robotic beehive, powered by deep reinforcement machine learning, with multiple cameras and sensors, providing advanced monitoring to the beekeeper along with remote control, removing the need of manual inspections and making beekeeping more ethical.
