State of beekeeping

Honeybees are cute super-organisms that we want to take care of ❤️. They are key to natural pollination and our food supply. However, hobby beekeeping is challenging as it requires constant monitoring to keep track of health, resources, attacks, reproduction, and swarming states.

Traditional beekeeping involves hard physical labor. On top of that, bees also suffer. Unpredictable weather, monocultural crops, pesticide use, viral and bacterial infections, birds, mites, rodents, bears, wasps - all of these factors affect the colony, which is constantly engaged in building, cleaning, reproduction, growth, and feeding. When you add constant inspections by the beekeeper, it leads to colony stress and loss of bees.

Unethical treatment of bees is prevalent. The lack of technological advancement, on one hand, leads some beekeepers to count mites using medieval alcohol washing tests that kill hundreds of bees, justifying it as saving the colony.

Industrialization and narrow specialization can lead to exploitation. Monocultural fields, lawns, golf fields result in a lack of pollen diversity. Industrial beekeepers, in turn, use trucks to move colonies around fields for pollination. This practice drains the lifespan of worker bees and creates unhealthy congestion of colonies, increasing health risks.

Existing modern solutions on the market are inadequate because they focus on low-cost temperature and sound sensors that provide insufficient information. Others focus only on one area - finding mites or finding queen, or counting bees at the gate. Alternative solutions target industrial beekeeping and focus on the unhealthy congestion of multiple bee colonies inside a shipping container.

Gratheon Open Beehive

Our long-term vision is a single-colony autonomous robotic beehive, powered by deep reinforcement machine learning, with multiple cameras and sensors, providing advanced monitoring to the beekeeper along with remote control, removing the need of manual inspections and making beekeeping more ethical.

Features

We want to achieve:

  • Monitoring. Give better insights on colony life by analyzing nursing and field bees demographics
    • ✅ Upload images of beehive frames
    • ✅ Detection of bees on a hive frame based on a photo
    • ✅ Analyze brood development and hive resources
    • ⏳ Stream video of gateway
    • ⏳ Improved detection of queen presense
    • ⏳ Detection of swarming based on queen cups
    • ⏳ Detection of field bee loss based on gate traffic video (bees coming in/out count)
    • ⏳ Support low-level sensor APIs (temperature, humidity, weight)
    • ⏳ Analyze audio for swarming and anomalies
  • Longevity of bee colony and individual worker bees.
    • ⏳ Detect hornets at the gate by video
    • ⏳ Detect varroa destructor mites
    • ⏳ Detect deseases based on bee morphography
  • Autonomous hive
    • ⏳ Use robotic box and frame actuators
    • ⏳ Gate control, depending on hornets & weather
    • ⏳ Ventilation assistance based on temperature/humidity
  • Modern digital app
    • ✅ Visually manage beehive and its frames using a web app
    • ✅ Plantnet integration with list of local plants
    • ✅ Weather forecast around apiary
    • ⏳ Timeline of inspections with a graph
